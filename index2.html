About LNYF

Lunar New Year Festival (LNYF) is a student-run organization at Washington University in St. Louis and the annual performance of the same name produced to celebrate the Lunar New Year and promote the awareness of different facets of Asian culture. The show, which is performed three times over two days at Edison Theatre in the Mallinckrodt Center on WashU's campus, is a completely student-run production involving students from various cultural groups on campus. At the same time, it allows WashU students to engage with multiple St. Louis communities by exploring Asian heritage from a traditional as well as a modern perspective.

Through exciting performances ranging from acting to dancing to martial arts, people of all backgrounds can enjoy and celebrate the Lunar New Year together at WashU. While LNYF makes its mark through the final production showcasing various facets of Asian culture, it hopes to make its mark as more than just another campus cultural show. LNYF seeks to leave a prolonged impact upon its viewers through our skit and philanthropy. In the skit, we seek to acknowledge and address important issues to the Asian American community that not only affect Asian Americans, but all people across the globe. Additionally, each year, LNYF chooses a different philanthropy to support to not only impact the WashU community, but the St. Louis community as well. This year we have chosen the philanthropy Variety.

Philanthropy

Variety, the children’s charity, has been providing services to children with special needs in St. Louis for more than 80 years. The organization promotes the need to focus on the children’s abilities by providing them with opportunities to demonstrate their potential. Variety offers medical equipment, various therapeutic programs, summer and day camps, Variety theatre and the Children’s chorus to help children redefine what they are capable of. Their philosophy emphasizes the importance of celebrating what the children CAN do, and supplying them platforms to further cultivate and explore their passions. The impact that Variety has had on the St. Louis community is truly immeasurable. LNYF is extremely proud to support, and be a part of the wonderful work that they have accomplished.

Learn more here: https://varietystl.org/

Cheesiest pickup line: You won't be hearing any from me °◡°

Current issue I feel strongly about: This past summer, Kim Kardashian announced details for a new business for a shape-wear line (something like Spandex) called "Kimono." At the time, I had just flown back to St. Louis after having the privilege of traveling around Japan for the first time. Having studied Japanese at WashU and been continuously interested in the depth of Japanese traditions, I felt frustrated that such blatant cultural appropriation could still exist in 2019. Though it is not my own culture to defend, I think cultural appropriation is a prevalent issue all around the world that happens across many cultures. It is important to recognize that cultural appropriation is usually done without malicious intent and stems from ignorance of the lives of people around the world. In connection to LNYF, I hope the performances and other cultural facets we host around campus will help increase general cultural literacy. On the note of Kim K, even the mayor of Kyoto, Daisaku Kadokawa, posted a letter to her on his Facebook page "asking [her] to re-consider your decision of using the name Kimono." She has since said that she will change the name of her brand venture.
